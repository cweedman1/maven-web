Nursing Home Deficiency Examples

Nursing home deficiencies are citations issued during surveys when facilities fail to meet regulatory standards. Understanding common deficiencies helps facilities improve compliance and reduce risk.

This guide provides real-world examples of deficiencies and how they are corrected.

Example 1: Failure to Maintain Safe Environment

Deficiency: Hazardous conditions such as wet floors or blocked exits.
Cause: Inadequate safety checks and staff awareness.
Correction: Implemented safety protocols and staff training.
Monitoring: Daily environmental audits.

Example 2: Medication Errors

Deficiency: Incorrect medication dosage administered.
Cause: Breakdown in medication verification process.
Correction: Reinforced medication administration procedures.
Monitoring: Ongoing medication audits and staff review.

Example 3: Inadequate Documentation

Deficiency: Missing or incomplete resident records.
Cause: Inconsistent documentation practices.
Correction: Standardized documentation workflows.
Monitoring: Weekly documentation audits.

Common Patterns in Deficiencies

  • Process failures
  • Lack of staff training
  • Poor documentation practices
  • Inconsistent monitoring
